# Frameworks, hooks, and CTAs

What to deploy when writing or punching up marketing copy. Pick one body framework, one hook, one CTA. Don't stack them or show the structure.

## Body frameworks

**AIDA** — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action. The default for ads and landing sections.
- Attention: the hook.
- Interest: why it's relevant to this reader now.
- Desire: the specific outcome they get (use an approved proof point).
- Action: the one CTA.

**PAS** — Problem, Agitate, Solution. Strong for cold email and pain-driven posts.
- Problem: name the reader's actual problem in their words.
- Agitate: the cost of leaving it unsolved — concrete, not catastrophizing.
- Solution: your offer as the obvious fix, with one CTA.

**BAB** — Before, After, Bridge. Good for transformation stories and case studies.
- Before: the painful status quo.
- After: the specific better state.
- Bridge: how you get them there.

Pick by what you have: a sharp pain → PAS; a clear transformation with a real result → BAB; a straightforward offer → AIDA.

## Hooks (first line)

A hook earns the second line. Keep it specific and back it with something real. Patterns that work when grounded:

- **Concrete result:** "We cut deploy time from 40 minutes to 6." (only if true and approved)
- **Counter-intuitive claim:** "More monitoring made our incidents worse." (then explain why)
- **Specific moment:** "The pager went off at 3am on launch day."
- **Named tension:** "Everyone says ship fast. Nobody says how to sleep after."

Avoid empty-hook tells (these are bait, not hooks): "What if I told you…", "Here's what nobody tells you…", ALL-CAPS words, "Read that again.", triple rhetorical questions, cliché proverbs ("Work smarter, not harder"). They open a gap with no real payoff and readers know it.

## CTAs (one, specific, low-friction)

- Reply-based: "Reply 'guide' and I'll send the checklist."
- Specific time: "Free Tuesday at 2pm ET?" (email)
- Single link with reason: "Full teardown here: [link]" (one line of why)
- Soft for top-of-funnel social: "Curious how you'd handle step 3."

Cut: "Thoughts?", "Agree?", "Drop a comment 👇", "Let me know if interested", stacked multiple asks, "DM me / comment / share / follow" combos. One ask.

## When punching up flat copy

The claims are already correct — your job is delivery, not invention.
1. Replace a flat opening line with a grounded hook from above.
2. Restructure the body onto AIDA/PAS/BAB without adding claims.
3. Collapse multiple asks into one CTA.
4. Vary sentence length; cut filler words.
5. If the copy needs a proof point it doesn't have, leave `[ADD PROOF]` — do not invent one.

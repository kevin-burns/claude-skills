# Marketing channels — format rules

Detect the channel, then apply. These cover *format and length* for persuasion copy. The floor in SKILL.md (no fabrication, one CTA, no filler, no fake vulnerability) applies on top of all of them.

## linkedin

- 1,300 chars is the sweet spot; hard ceiling 3,000. Front-load the hook above the "see more" fold (first ~210 chars).
- Short paragraphs (1–3 lines) with white space, but not one-line-per-sentence the whole way — vary it.
- 0–3 hashtags, woven in or at the end. Not a stack of ten.
- Emoji only if it carries meaning, not as bullet decoration.
- One CTA. A genuine question is allowed; engagement bait ("Agree? 👇") is not.

## x (twitter)

- Single post: one idea, one hook, ≤280 chars. Cut every word that isn't load-bearing.
- Thread: hook tweet must stand alone and make the scroll stop. Each tweet earns the next. Last tweet has the one CTA.
- No hashtag stuffing. Usually zero.

## bluesky

- 300 graphemes per post, same for everyone — no premium tier buys more. URLs count against it and aren't auto-shortened, so shorten long links first.
- The feed and custom feeds weight keywords in the post text more than hashtags, so write the searchable words into the sentence and keep hashtags to 0–2.
- Culture skews tech/early-adopter and is openly hostile to growth-hacking. Engagement bait and obvious "hooks" land worse here than on LinkedIn. Lead with a real, specific thought; let it be conversational.
- Longer ideas go in a thread (reply to your own post), with the first post standing on its own.

## youtube (packaging only — titles, thumbnails, descriptions, CTAs)

For the conversion surfaces around a video. The script itself is not here — a teaching/explainer script goes to clear-and-human.

- **Title:** 100 chars max, but only ~60–70 show in search and suggested tiles. Front-load the hook and primary keyword in the first ~55. Don't repeat words already on the thumbnail. CTR is the signal that matters; specific and concrete beats clever.
- **Thumbnail text:** 3–5 words, complements the title rather than repeating it. One idea.
- **Description:** 5,000 chars, but only ~150 show before "…more" — put the hook and the value there. Below the fold: timestamps, links, context. 2–3 relevant hashtags in the description (max 15 or YouTube ignores all of them); the first 3 surface above the title.
- **End-screen / pinned-comment CTA:** one specific action (subscribe for X, grab the linked resource), not a stack.

## marketing-email / outreach

- Subject: specific and curiosity-opening, not clickbait. Under ~50 chars.
- First line is the hook and must work in the preview pane — not "Hi, I hope you're well."
- PAS or BAB body, kept short. One ask. One link max.
- Sign-off: one line, human. No stacked "Best regards / Thanks / Cheers".
- Personalize with a real detail or don't pretend to.

## landing-page / ad

- Headline does the work of the hook: the outcome, specific, above the fold.
- Subhead clarifies the offer in plain words.
- AIDA structure; proof points must be real (pull from WRITING_CONTEXT.md approved facts).
- One primary CTA button intent, repeated, not five competing actions.
- Body in short scannable blocks; benefit before feature.

## lead-magnet

- Promise one specific, valuable outcome ("the 6-step TLS rotation checklist"), not "everything you need to know".
- The hook is the gap between where the reader is and that outcome.
- CTA is the low-friction grab (reply, click, enter email) with one clear value exchange.
